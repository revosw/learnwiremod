Finding it in the menu

Open up the spawn menu with Q, and have a look at the Wire tab. Under Chips, Gates you'll find Expression 2.

You can create, open, copy, rename and delete files. Folders are different, you can create them but not rename, nor delete them. Issue on Github

Editor overview

When you select the tool, close the spawn menu and right click to open the editor. Optionally, you can click the button on the bottom of the tool's spawn menu and open the editor there, but I have personally never used that button.

Auto completion

I recommend:

  • Auto completion ON
  • More info ON
  • Auto completion control style DEFAULT
  • Highlight word after AC use OFF
Auto completion both helps you correctly type functions, and makes you discover other functions.


Other options

I recommend:

  • New tab on open ON
  • Save tabs on close ON
  • Open old tabs on load ON
  • Show caret position YOU DECIDE YOURSELF
  • Highlight copies of selected word ON
  • Enable clicking outside editor OFF

You'll want to disable clicking outside the editor because of this happening when you resize the window:



Settings - Expression 2

Now you need to navigate to the second tab of the settings page.

Clientside Expression 2 options

I recommend:

  • Auto indentation ON
  • Concmd OFF
  • Concmd whitelist EMPTY (it won't do anything when concmd is off anyway)
  • Expression 2 block comment style DOESN'T SEEM TO DO ANYTHING
  • Ops/cpu usage sync ON

I like auto indenting because it automatically helps in organizing the code. You'll understand what I mean later.

By checking the concmd checkbox, your expressions can run console commands on your behalf. Very useful if you know what you're doing, but we'll leave it off for now.

Settings - Remote updater

In this menu, you can manage your expressions remotely. The feature is very useful if, for example, your expression is far away from you or deep within a contraption and you want to edit it.

The menu benefits server admins too, as not all expressions are coded with good intents. Some people who code expressions do everything in their might to be as annoying as possible, playing loud sounds everywhere and trying to crash the server. A person who code expressions with evil intents is what most people call a "mingebag", and you don't want to call everyone around you that. The expression of mingebags can then be found, and an admin can ban/kick/warn those who have mingebag-expressions. In the picture below, we can see a player blinder. Sounds annoying being blinded, don't you think?

However, there's a small downside to it. Server owners can with the click of a button download your expression. You will be notified of it, but if you're working on a grand project, it's not a great feeling having your code copied like that.
To server owners: Don't be a jerk Please respect expression programmers' privacy and don't just hammer the download buttons to steal/copy other people's code. Though there are no executive powers protecting a right of privacy, all I ask of you is to keep a decent moral standard.
