The wait is over

Finally we are going to start learning the actual language. But I think it was important that I placed all that tool-stuff on its own page, rather than stretching it throughout the whole basics section. From now on, most of what we're going to see is code.

It is a convention in programming that the first thing a new programmer learns is how to output the string "Hello World!" to a console (or in our case, the chat). "But wait," you say. String?

String - any* text, numbers or other characters or symbols delimited by quotation marks

"Hello World!" is a string. "#123" is a string. Why? Because the characters are delimited by, or contained within, a set of quotation marks. But there is a catch, which is why there is an asterisk. I'll come back to that later.

Now we just need the final piece in our "Hello World!"-formula, namely the print() function (don't worry, I'll explain functions later). It now looks like this:

print("Hello World!")

And we got ourselves a salutation to mother earth. Beautiful.

But where did print() come from? Throughout this whole Expression 2 course, we are going to cover a LOT of functions. You can find the functions under the , located in the top right section of the editor. When the time is right, I will explain how to use the E2Helper.
